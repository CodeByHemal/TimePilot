# TimePlot – Smart Student Productivity Hub

## 📌 Overview

TimePlot is a modern, student-first productivity platform designed to centralize academic workflows—**timetables, schedules, tasks, deadlines, and notices**—into a single, intuitive mobile experience. The system features two dedicated environments: a **Student Panel** for consumption and productivity, and a **Faculty Panel** for creation, management, and broadcasting.

Inspired by a clean, card-driven UI (as shown in the design), the app emphasizes **clarity, prioritization, and real-time visibility** of academic activities.

---

## ✨ Product Experience (Based on UI Design)

### 🎯 Student Experience

* **Today’s Momentum (Home Dashboard):** Snapshot of today’s schedule, urgent tasks, and recent notices
* **Campus Companion Feed:** Personalized cards for assignments, updates, and quick actions
* **Daily Briefing:** Curated list of tasks and priorities for the day
* **Weekly Itinerary:** Scrollable weekly view with subjects, sessions, and time blocks

### 🛠️ Faculty Experience

* **Faculty Portal Dashboard:** Overview of student progress, pending tasks, and recent broadcasts
* **Create New Task:** Structured task creation with subtasks, priority, and deadlines
* **Broadcast Notice:** Send announcements to targeted groups with attachments
* **Schedule Builder:** Create and manage academic schedules with precision

### 🔐 Entry Flow

* **Login Screen:** Clean authentication UI
* **Splash Screen:** Branded app entry with smooth transition

## 🎨 Design Principles

* **Card-based layout** for clarity and modularity
* **Minimal and intuitive UI** for non-technical users
* **Visual priority indicators** for urgent actions
* **Consistent typography and spacing** for readability
* **Smooth navigation** across student and faculty flows

---

## 🔒 Constraints & Considerations

🧩 User-Friendly Design
The application must be intuitive and easy to use, even for non-technical users.
🔍 Flexible Filtering
Should support filtering of schedules, tasks, and events based on date and category for better organization.
🎯 Clear Visual Prioritization
Must provide strong visual indicators (e.g., colors, labels, highlights) to distinguish between high, medium, and low priority items.
